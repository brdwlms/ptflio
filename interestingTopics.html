The Internet of Things

From a recent Informative Speech assignment, I discussed the Internet of Things.
The Internet of Things is a fascinating topic because it has so much potential! We know, that as technology progresses the capacity for it to adapt to an ever-changing environment is what will make it practical.
--- The definition is simple, devices that exist in a network share their experiences with one another and unlitmately, the user. The internet is, in a very real way, connected to the physical world through various sensors. For more info check out this increidible video,

What is the Internet of Things? And why should you care? | Benson Hougland | TEDxTemecula (YouTube Link)

What is most appealing to me is the amount of efficiency that can be achieved in this system. When every device has the ability to communicate, there is no factor of human error. No switch is left on and nothing will conserve more energy than necessary. This will influence our future for the better, I do feel that it must become more adaptable in peoples lives so it can be accepted.

3D Printing with OctoPi

I decided to automate my 3D Printer.
In mid 2019, I took responibility with a shop on Etsy selling 3D Printed sculptures. I ended up running my printer 24/7 while at school and felt on edge leaving it on with no one to monitor. I followed a YouTube tutorial and set up OctoPi with plugins like OctoPrint Anywhere and The Spagetti Detective all controled by a Raspberry Pi ZeroW and my Wifi Network. With these plugins, I was able to view and control my printer from school, The Spagetti Detective would even use AI to monitor the print and send me an email if it is starting to look like plastic spagetti.




SpaceX and Virgin Orbit Tour 2019

Our engineering club was fortunate enough to arrange tours with two revolutionary companies, SpaceX and Virgin Orbit.

SpaceX
This tour was absolutely incredible. We were blown inspired by the workflow and amount of technology present. Our group learned about the processes that go into developing and testing new systems as well as how these tests are monitored through the control room. Yet another great feature was seeing the autonomous Tesla Model S security cars and the table Elon Musk sometimes eats at.

Virgin Orbit
As an up-and-coming small-satellite launching company, only a few years old, Virgin Orbit already has so much to offer in the industry and a great amount of potential in its future. I was delighted to meet the president and CEO Dan Hart while touring. This experience was very valuble to everyone of us, I was pleased to show our engineering club how Virgin Orbit develops their own rocket thruster cones in-house using a Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) 3D Printer. I have been keeping up with their news and am looking forward to attending their first tests!
